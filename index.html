Who We Are

Built on an old idea — that gau seva is a duty, not a favour.

Shree Gau Kalyan Foundation began as a small effort by a handful of villagers in Mathura, pooling money to treat one injured cow left by the roadside.

Today we work with a network of gaushalas across six states, but the work hasn't changed — find a cow that's sick, old, abandoned, or simply has nowhere left to go, and give her a place where she's fed, treated and never sent away again.

  • Rescue & Rehabilitation

    Field teams rescue injured and stray cows from highways, markets and city outskirts.

  • Daily Feed & Veterinary Care

    Fodder, clean water and on-site vets at every gaushala, every single day of the year.

  • Permanent Shelter

    Once a cow enters our care, she stays for life — no resale, no abandonment.

Why This Matters

India has millions of stray cattle. Most gaushalas can't keep up.

As dairy yields drop, ageing and unproductive cows are increasingly turned loose. Without intervention, most don't survive their first year on the street.

Highway Injuries

Stray cows are frequently struck by vehicles on national highways and left untreated for days.

Plastic & Waste Ingestion

Foraging in city garbage, many cows ingest plastic that causes fatal digestive blockages.

No Place to Go

Government shelters are overcrowded; many cows have nowhere left once they're abandoned.

Common Questions

Before you donate

Where exactly does my donation go?

Funds are pooled and distributed across our network of 11 partner gaushalas based on immediate need — fodder, veterinary supplies, shelter repair and rescue transport.

Is my donation tax-deductible?

Yes, donations are eligible under Section 80G. Mention your PAN while donating and write to us afterward, and we'll send a receipt for your tax filing.

Can I donate every month?

Absolutely — many of our supporters set up a recurring monthly contribution. You can choose this option directly on the donation page.

How do I know the cows are actually cared for?

We share monthly photo and video updates from our gaushalas with all registered donors, and welcome visits by appointment.
